| Channel | Details |

| Email | complaints@finserve.eu |
| Phone | +48 22 100 2010 (Mon–Fri, 08:00–18:00 CET) |
| Online form | portal.finserve.eu → "Submit a Complaint" |
| Written letter | FinServe S.A., Complaint Department, ul. Europejska 12, 00-950 Warsaw, Poland |

Complaints may be submitted in English, Polish, German, Spanish, or French.

---

## 2. Required Information

To ensure timely processing, every complaint must include:

1. **Client identification:** Full name, client number (found on your loan agreement or portal account), and contact email or phone number.
2. **Product reference:** Loan agreement number or invoice financing facility number.
3. **Description of the issue:** A clear description of the problem, including dates, amounts, and any relevant reference numbers.
4. **Desired resolution:** What outcome the client is seeking (e.g., fee reversal, correction of account data, compensation).
5. **Supporting documents (if applicable):** Copies of correspondence, payment confirmations, screenshots, or other evidence.

Complaints that are missing critical information (client number or product reference) will be acknowledged, and the client will be asked to provide the missing details within 5 business days. The SLA clock pauses until the information is received.

---

## 3. Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

| Stage | Timeframe |
|---|---|
| Acknowledgement of receipt | Within 1 business day |
| First substantive response | Within 5 business days |
| Full resolution (standard complaints) | Within 15 business days |
| Full resolution (complex complaints requiring investigation) | Within 30 business days |
| Notification if extended beyond 15 business days | By business day 15 at the latest |

If FinServe cannot resolve the complaint within 30 business days, the client will receive a written explanation of the delay and an updated timeline.

---

## 4. Internal Escalation Path

### Level 1 — Client Support Agent
The assigned agent reviews the complaint, gathers information, and attempts to resolve it within 5 business days. The agent has authority to:
- Reverse fees up to EUR 50.
- Correct data entry errors.
- Provide clarification and explanation.

### Level 2 — Client Support Team Lead
If the agent cannot resolve the issue, or if the client is not satisfied with the Level 1 response, the complaint is escalated to the Team Lead. The Team Lead has authority to:
- Reverse fees up to EUR 200.
- Approve goodwill gestures up to EUR 100.
- Coordinate with Credit & Operations or Finance departments.
- Deadline: additional 5 business days from escalation.

### Level 3 — Head of Client Support
Complaints involving potential regulatory issues, amounts exceeding EUR 200, or repeated complaints from the same client are escalated to the Head of Client Support. Authority includes:
- Full fee reversal regardless of amount.
- Compensation offers up to EUR 500.
- Engagement of Risk & Compliance if regulatory breach is suspected.
- Deadline: additional 5 business days from escalation.

### Level 4 — Chief Operating Officer
Complaints that remain unresolved or involve systemic issues are referred to the COO for final internal decision. This is the final internal escalation level. Deadline: 5 business days.

---

## 5. Client Appeal Rights

If the client is not satisfied with FinServe's final response, they have the right to:

1. **Financial Ombudsman:** File a complaint with the relevant national financial ombudsman or dispute resolution body (e.g., Rzecznik Finansowy in Poland, BaFin complaint desk in Germany). FinServe will provide the relevant contact details in the final response letter.
2. **Court proceedings:** The client may pursue legal remedies in the competent court of their jurisdiction.
3. **European Online Dispute Resolution (ODR):** Retail clients may use the EU ODR platform at https://ec.europa.eu/consumers/odr.

FinServe will include information about external appeal options in every final response to a complaint.

---

## 6. Record Keeping

All complaints and related correspondence are recorded in FinServe's complaint management system (CMS) and retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of resolution. Complaint data is used for internal quality analysis and regulatory reporting.
