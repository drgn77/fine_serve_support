# FinServe — Late Payment Policy

**Document version:** 2.3  
**Effective date:** 1 January 2025  
**Approved by:** Chief Financial Officer, FinServe S.A.

---

## 1. Definition of a Late Payment

A payment is considered late when the full instalment amount due (principal + interest) is not credited to FinServe's designated account by 23:59 CET on the contractual due date. Partial payments are applied first to accrued interest, then to principal, then to any outstanding fees.

---

## 2. Grace Period

FinServe provides a **5-calendar-day grace period** after the contractual due date. During the grace period:
- No late payment fee is charged.
- No penalty interest accrues.
- No negative credit bureau reporting is made.
- The client receives an automated SMS and email reminder on day 1 and day 4 of the grace period.

If the full payment is received within the grace period, the account is treated as current with no adverse consequences.

---

## 3. Penalty Interest

If payment is not received by the end of the grace period (day 6 after the due date), penalty interest begins to accrue on the overdue amount at a rate of **the contractual interest rate plus 4 percentage points per annum**.

Example: If the contractual rate is 8.5%, penalty interest is 12.5% per annum on the overdue amount, calculated daily from the first day after the grace period until the overdue amount is fully repaid.

---

## 4. Fees

| Fee | Amount | When applied |
|---|---|---|
| Late payment fee | EUR 15 | Charged once per missed instalment, on the first business day after the grace period expires |
| Second reminder fee | EUR 25 | Charged if payment remains overdue after 30 calendar days |
| Formal demand letter fee | EUR 40 | Charged when a formal written demand is sent (after 60 calendar days overdue) |

All fees are added to the outstanding balance and disclosed in the client's account statement.

---

## 5. Collection Stages

### Stage 1 — Soft Collection (Days 6–30)
- The assigned Client Support agent contacts the client by phone and email to discuss the overdue payment.
- The agent explores reasons for non-payment and offers solutions (payment plan, temporary deferral).
- Frequency: at least 2 contact attempts per week.
- The client may request a one-time payment deferral of up to 14 days (available once per 12-month period, no fee).

### Stage 2 — Formal Collection (Days 31–60)
- The case is escalated to the Collections Team within Credit & Operations.
- A second reminder letter is sent by email and registered post.
- The Collections Team contacts the client to negotiate a repayment arrangement.
- The client's account is flagged as "overdue" in internal systems.
- If the client agrees to a repayment arrangement, the arrangement is documented and monitored. A restructuring fee of EUR 50 applies.

### Stage 3 — Formal Demand (Days 61–90)
- A formal demand letter is sent, setting a 14-day deadline for full repayment or entry into a restructuring agreement.
- The client is informed that failure to respond may result in termination of the agreement and referral to external collection.
- Credit bureau reporting: the overdue status is reported to relevant credit bureaus (BIK in Poland, SCHUFA in Germany, or equivalent) after 60 days.

### Stage 4 — Agreement Termination and External Collection (Day 91+)
- If no payment or arrangement is made by day 90, FinServe may terminate the loan agreement with 30 days' written notice.
- The full outstanding balance (principal, accrued interest, penalty interest, and fees) becomes immediately due.
- The case is referred to an external debt collection agency or to FinServe's legal department for court proceedings.
- Collateral enforcement (if applicable) is initiated in accordance with the loan agreement and applicable law.

---

## 6. Debt Restructuring

Clients experiencing financial difficulty may request debt restructuring **before** the account reaches Stage 4. Restructuring options include:

1. **Extended repayment term:** The loan term may be extended by up to 12 months, reducing the monthly instalment. Available for SME loans and retail credit lines.
2. **Temporary interest-only payments:** The client pays only interest for a period of up to 3 months. Principal payments resume after the interest-only period. Available once per agreement.
3. **Reduced instalment plan:** Monthly instalments may be reduced by up to 40% for a period of up to 6 months, with the difference added to the remaining term.

### Conditions for Restructuring
- The client must submit a written request to support@finserve.eu or through the portal.
- The request must include a brief explanation of the financial difficulty and a proposed repayment plan.
- Restructuring is subject to approval by a Senior Credit Analyst (loans up to EUR 100,000) or the Credit Committee (loans above EUR 100,000).
- Processing time: 5 business days from receipt of a complete request.
- Restructuring fee: EUR 50 per restructuring event.
- A restructured account is marked as "restructured" in internal records and credit bureau reports.
