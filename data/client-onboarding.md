# FinServe — Client Onboarding Procedure

**Document version:** 2.1  
**Effective date:** 1 January 2025  
**Approved by:** Head of Risk & Compliance, FinServe S.A.

---

## 1. Overview

This document describes the onboarding process for new FinServe clients, including required documentation, KYC/AML verification, and approval timelines. The procedure applies to both retail clients and SME clients.

---

## 2. Required Documents

### 2.1 Retail Clients

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| Government-issued ID | Valid passport or national identity card (must be valid for at least 3 months) |
| Proof of income | Last 3 months of payslips, or most recent annual tax declaration, or pension statement |
| Proof of address | Utility bill, bank statement, or official correspondence dated within the last 3 months |
| Bank account details | IBAN of the client's primary bank account (for disbursement and repayment) |

### 2.2 SME Clients

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| Company registration certificate | Issued by the relevant national registry (e.g., KRS in Poland, Handelsregister in Germany) — must be current within 6 months |
| Financial statements | Audited or unaudited financial statements for the last 2 completed fiscal years |
| Bank statements | Last 6 months of business bank statements |
| Government-issued ID of authorised signatory | Valid passport or national identity card |
| Proof of business address | Utility bill, lease agreement, or official correspondence dated within the last 3 months |
| Beneficial ownership declaration | Declaration identifying all individuals holding 25% or more of the company's shares or voting rights |
| Business description | Brief description of business activities, main clients, and purpose of the requested financing (1–2 pages) |

## 3. KYC/AML Verification Steps

FinServe performs the following checks as part of the onboarding process, in compliance with EU Anti-Money Laundering Directives (AMLD5/AMLD6) and local regulations.

### Step 1 — Document Verification (Day 1)
- All submitted documents are checked for authenticity, completeness, and validity.
- Identity documents are verified against the original or certified copy.
- For SMEs, company registration data is cross-referenced with the national business registry.

### Step 2 — Identity Verification (Day 1–2)
- Retail clients: Identity verified via electronic ID verification (eIDV) or video identification.
- SME authorised signatories: Same process as retail clients.
- Beneficial owners holding 25% or more are identified and verified.

### Step 3 — Sanctions and PEP Screening (Day 2)
- All individuals (clients, signatories, beneficial owners) are screened against:
  - EU consolidated sanctions list.
  - UN Security Council sanctions list.
  - National sanctions lists (relevant jurisdiction).
  - Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) databases.
  - Adverse media databases.
- Screening is automated via FinServe's compliance platform. Hits are reviewed manually by a Risk & Compliance analyst within 1 business day.

### Step 4 — Risk Assessment (Day 2–3)
- Each client is assigned a risk rating: **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**.
- Risk rating is based on: country of residence/registration, business sector, transaction volume, PEP status, and source of funds.
- Low-risk clients proceed to approval. Medium- and high-risk clients require enhanced due diligence (EDD), which may include additional documentation requests and a senior compliance officer review.

### Step 5 — Credit Assessment (Day 3–5 for SME; Day 1–2 for Retail)
- Creditworthiness is assessed based on submitted financials, credit bureau data, and internal scoring models.
- SME loans: A credit memo is prepared by a Credit Analyst and reviewed by the Credit Committee (for loans above EUR 100,000) or a Senior Credit Analyst (for loans up to EUR 100,000).
- Retail credit lines: Automated scoring with manual review for borderline cases.

## 4. Process Timeline

| Stage | Retail Clients | SME Clients |
|---|---|---|
| Document submission | Day 0 | Day 0 |
| Document and identity verification | Day 1 | Day 1–2 |
| Sanctions/PEP screening | Day 1 | Day 2 |
| Risk assessment | Day 1–2 | Day 2–3 |
| Credit assessment | Day 1–2 | Day 3–5 |
| Approval decision | Day 2 | Day 5 |
| Agreement signing | Day 2–3 | Day 5–7 |
| Funds disbursement | Day 3 (1 business day after signing) | Day 7–9 (2 business days after signing) |

**Total timeline:** Retail clients: approximately 3 business days. SME clients: approximately 5–9 business days.

## 5. Possible Rejection Reasons

An application may be rejected for any of the following reasons:

1. **Incomplete or invalid documentation** — missing or expired documents that are not provided within 10 business days of the request.
2. **Failed identity verification** — inability to verify the client's identity through available channels.
3. **Sanctions or PEP hit** — confirmed match on a sanctions list or unresolved PEP flag requiring prohibition of the business relationship.
4. **Negative credit assessment** — credit score below the minimum threshold, debt-to-income ratio exceeding 50% (retail) or debt service coverage ratio below 1.2x (SME).
5. **High-risk assessment without mitigation** — client rated as high-risk with insufficient documentation or explanation of source of funds.
6. **Adverse media findings** — credible reports of fraud, financial crime, or other disqualifying conduct.
7. **Business sector exclusion** — FinServe does not finance businesses in the following sectors: weapons and ammunition, gambling (unless fully licensed), adult entertainment, cryptocurrency mining, and tobacco manufacturing.

Rejected applicants receive a written explanation within 2 business days and may reapply after 90 days or once the rejection reason has been resolved.

---

## 6. Post-Approval Steps

After approval:
1. The client receives the loan or credit line agreement by email (or through the portal) for electronic signature.
2. The signed agreement is countersigned by FinServe within 1 business day.
3. The client's account is activated in the core banking system.
4. Funds are disbursed to the client's designated bank account:
   - Retail credit lines: within 1 business day of activation.
   - SME loans: within 2 business days of activation.
   - Invoice financing: facility activated within 1 business day; individual invoices funded within 24 hours of submission.
5. The client receives a welcome email with portal login credentials, repayment schedule, and key contact details.

---

## 7. Ongoing Obligations

- **Periodic KYC review:** Low-risk clients are reviewed every 36 months, medium-risk every 24 months, and high-risk every 12 months.
- **Change of circumstances:** Clients must notify FinServe within 30 days of any material change (change of address, ownership, legal status, or financial condition).
- **Continuous monitoring:** FinServe conducts ongoing transaction monitoring and periodic sanctions screening throughout the client relationship.
